Futures

Hyprland tiles beautifully on a flat monitor today. In five years it's an XR headset; in twenty, there may be no screen at all. KOOMPI's shell extends into space: windows become objects in a room, workspaces become actual rooms you walk between, notifications become spatial sounds, and the AI becomes a presence — a quiet glow in the corner, or just a voice.

Default: a soft, breathing glow at the room's edge — present but never in your sightline. Speak and it answers from there; it never blocks your work or stares back.

No-screen mode — ambient + voice only
When there's no display, KOOMPI is a presence in the space: a notification is a soft tone from your left; the briefing is spoken; a decision is a question in the air. The shell becomes sound, light, and language.
From tiling to rooms KOOMPI

Hyprland's window model extends naturally into space: workspaces become rooms you physically move between, and windows persist where you placed them — spatial memory instead of a flat z-order.

The AI as a presence, not a popup

In space the assistant has a place — a glow at the edge, a voice, or an avatable shape. It stays out of your line of sight and answers from where it lives, never intruding into the work.

Designed for the screenless future

The same OS degrades gracefully to sound and light when there's no display at all. The shell is an idea — context, intent, presence — not a rectangle of pixels.
