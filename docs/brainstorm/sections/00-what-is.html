WHY
the belief
In the AI era, whoever holds your data owns your mind's prosthetic.
Cloud assistants read your files, your messages, your habits — and remember them on someone else's servers, under someone else's law, for someone else's business model. The more useful AI becomes, the more it knows; the more it knows, the more dangerous that arrangement gets. We believe a person's digital memory belongs to them the way their home does. So we build the opposite architecture: the model comes to the data, the data never goes to the model's owner.

HOW
five commitments
1 · One door out, with a ledger
Every byte that leaves passes one auditable egress gate. The Egress Ledger is the receipt; "0 bytes left this machine" is read from the kernel, not promised.
2 · Color is a contract
Emerald = local/safe, amber = consent, red = violation, indigo = AI, cyan = data. Never decoration — a privacy state you can read at a glance.
3 · Consent is the interaction model
Everything ships off. The AI proposes with reasons and confidence; you approve, override, or say "never again" — and undo survives reboots.
4 · Degrade local, never silent cloud
On weak hardware the model steps down and says so. It never quietly falls back to someone's API. Honesty over capability, always (ADR-0001).
5 · Runs on real hardware
The floor is an Intel UHD 620 classroom laptop, not a flagship GPU. Effects tier down gracefully; a 4 GB machine still gets a working AI.
+ Designed for 50 years
Memory that survives model churn, formats that outlive their apps, and an exit door at full fidelity — the Horizon sections take this to its conclusion.

🧭
The frame test — applied to every section below
Does it make the user more capable without taking their data? Does it state its privacy posture in pixels? Does it run on the hardware people own? Does it still make sense in year 30? If any answer is no, it doesn't ship.
north star
Why an OS, and why now KOOMPI

Assistants are becoming the interface to everything — and every incumbent ties that interface to their cloud. The window to establish a sovereign alternative is the same window in which habits form. An app can't fix this; only the layer that owns the index, the memory, and the egress path can. That layer is the OS.

Trust is the product

Big Tech cannot copy "your data never leaves" without dismantling their revenue. A small Cambodian company can — which turns KOOMPI's size and origin from a weakness into the moat. Every design in this document spends that trust budget carefully: visible states, auditable claims, reversible actions.

Ship v1, design for 100 years

The near goal is concrete: v1 "Naga" — base system, Context Engine, Assistant with memory, privacy floor — demoed at CODE-C 2026. The far goal shapes every choice on the way: an OS a person installs at 12 and still trusts at 62. Short-term proof, long-term frame.
