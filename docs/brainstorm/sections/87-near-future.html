✦ Capability gates — the mechanism this section exists for
v1 already ships when real, never on a date. The gate generalizes that to every future feature: name the external unlock and a measurable threshold; the feature ships when the gate passes. A feature without a gate row is fiction by definition. The registry lives in docs/blueprint.md §3.

Three calls only the founder can make
D-1 · The Home Node. One 16GB box per household serving models, index, and sync to thin clients — the implementable §86, and the RAM crisis is its business case. Default: prototype on an N100-class mini-PC before committing.
D-2 · SKU posture. 8GB honest floor vs 16GB premium positioning while RAM is 3–4× cost. Default: 8GB floor, honestly tiered.
D-3 · Khmer speech strategy. Fund the Cambodian speech-OSS community vs build in-house. Default: fund the community.

The floor rises slower than the hype KOOMPI

The RAM crisis keeps the modest machine on stage through at least 2028 — and Copilot+ abandons it. Designing beautifully for the hardware people actually own stops being a compromise and becomes the position no incumbent can take.

Adopt the protocol, invent the leash

MCP's victory is a gift: the app-bus, the intent layer, and the tool ecosystem arrive free. KOOMPI's entire invention budget goes where no one else spends theirs — consent, containment, and the audit trail around every capability call.

Gates, not dates

Khmer dictation at "CER ≤ 15% on a classroom mic" is engineering; "dialect-aware voice" without a corpus is fiction. The gate registry is how the Horizon earns its way into the Blueprint — one measured threshold at a time.
