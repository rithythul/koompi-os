The Household Constitution

Futures 1.x

At 1.x a household keeps a single plain-language policy-floor file — in Khmer or English — listing what the assistant may never do, must always do, and must ask about first. It is the human-readable law layer of the one Policy Engine, enforced mechanically by koompi-egress and the approval layer. Amendments that lower a floor wait out a cooling-off period before taking effect; amendments that raise a floor apply instantly. That asymmetry is the whole mechanism: impulse can add a guardrail but cannot remove one on the spot.

Floors go up instantly; they come down slowly. Until the cooling-off elapses, the old floor stands and the assistant cannot be argued out of it. Strengthening amendments skip the wait entirely.

Law, not settings KOOMPI

The floor file is plain language you wrote, in your own register of Khmer, enforced mechanically by koompi-egress. The document the assistant obeys is the document you can read aloud.

Cooling-off protects you from yourself

Any amendment that weakens a floor waits before taking effect; strengthening is instant. The asymmetry is the point — the 2am version of you cannot overrule the person who wrote the rule.
