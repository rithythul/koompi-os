The three dignity rules — hard-coded, not policy
🤲
Assist, always
Adapt the interface, surface the receipt, repeat without sighing. Help is unconditional and unremarkable.
🚫
Diagnose, never
The OS observes patterns; it does not name conditions. "Here's the receipt" — never "your memory is declining." Medicine belongs to doctors you choose.
🔒
Tell no one, without consent
Not your children, not your doctor, not an insurer. What the OS notices about your aging never crosses the egress gate unless you send it.
↩️ "Undo all adaptations" restores the 2056 baseline in one step. Dignity includes the right to refuse help.
No elderly mode KOOMPI

A toggle that announces "you are old now" is a small humiliation shipped as a feature. KOOMPI adapts the way aging actually happens — continuously, imperceptibly, over twenty years — and shows its work only when asked.

A prosthesis, not a nurse

When recall fades, the Memory Palace answers with receipts: what happened, when, with proof. It compensates for memory without ever commenting on it — capability support, not condition management.

Only a local OS can be trusted with this

Decline data is the most exploitable signal a person emits — to insurers, scammers, even family. Because every observation lives behind the egress gate, noticing and betraying are physically separate acts, and the second one never happens.
