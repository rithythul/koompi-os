reading order
A map you can hold KOOMPI

Eighty-three sections written in waves need a zoom-out before any zoom-in. One concise concept per section is the contract: if a section can't be said in one line, it isn't one thing yet — the same legibility we demand of the OS, demanded of its own blueprint.

Two maps, two jobs

The System Map keeps the machinery one — eight engines, three specs, no duplicate ask boxes. The Document Map keeps the narrative one — ten acts in reading order, descent and return. Same anti-silo instinct, applied to the document itself.

The map is maintained

Same rule as the System Map: a new section must take a cell here — find its act, earn its one-line concept — or it doesn't join the story. A map that drifts from the territory is worse than no map at all.
