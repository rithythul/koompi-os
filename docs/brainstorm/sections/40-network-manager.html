Resolution: the stricter posture always wins
Block ⊐ VPN-only ⊐ Allow. Privacy Lockdown wins — Travel's VPN stays armed underneath, so the instant lockdown lifts, traffic resumes through the tunnel, never in the clear. The user is told plainly, not silently overridden.
Postures form a lattice, not a list. The firewall takes the meet (∧) — never a last-writer-wins race that could leak a packet during the switch.

Sovereignty starts at the NIC KOOMPI

macOS and GNOME treat the firewall as an afterthought you configure once. KOOMPI inverts it: trust posture is the primary object, and connectivity is what posture permits. Every saved network, every app, every tunnel resolves to one enforced verdict before a packet moves — and that verdict is exactly what §28 logs.

One model, three views

The per-app rule you set, the live flow you watch, and the ledger row §28 records are the same fact written once. Windows 11 scatters this across Firewall, Resource Monitor, and Settings with no shared truth. KOOMPI's network-trust model is the single source §28 and §31 both consume.

The stricter posture always wins

Postures form a lattice; the firewall takes the meet. Travel's VPN and Privacy's lockdown never race — the strict rule applies instantly and the VPN stays armed underneath, so lifting lockdown resumes traffic through the tunnel, never in the clear. No last-writer-wins window where a packet could leak.

Ages by accretion, not reset

Trust posture is keyed to SSID + BSSID and persists for decades. A café you trusted in 2026 is still scoped correctly in 2046 — and if its BSSID changes underneath the name, KOOMPI treats it as new and asks again. A Lifetime OS remembers networks the way you do: by the place, not just the label.
