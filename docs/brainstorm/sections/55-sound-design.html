Sound Design

Sonic

The audio identity of KOOMPI. UI sonics, ambient focus tones, notification audio, and the boot/login signature — designed, restrained, and culturally resonant. The auditory companion to the motion and theme languages, rooted in a Khmer musical sensibility. Quiet by default: the system earns every sound it plays, because silence is the baseline and attention is the user's to spend.

Ageing across decades
The sound set is versioned (koompi-sounds 1.x) and the motif is sacred — like the Mac startup chime, the boot signature must survive 20 years untouched so a 2046 KOOMPI still sounds like home to someone who first heard it in school. Timbres can be re-recorded at higher fidelity; the melody never changes. Users can pin a theme version forever, so a redesign never steals the sound a person grew up with.
One instrument, not a soundboard KOOMPI

Every token is drawn from the Pinpeat ensemble's struck idiophones — roneat, kse diev, wood-block. The OS sounds like a single tuned instrument across success, error, notify, and capture. Windows and GNOME mix unrelated synthesized blips; KOOMPI commits to one timbral family so the system has a recognizable voice.

Silence is the default

First boot ships in near-silent "Travel" profile. Sound is opt-in, not opt-out. Quiet-by-default respects the user's attention and the people around them — a classroom of 40 KOOMPI laptops should never become 40 beeping machines.

Every sound is attributable

AI-originated events carry a faint indigo overtone, distinct from human-triggered sounds. Tied to §11 tool approval, you can hear when the AI acted versus when you did — transparency extended to the ear. No black-box beeps.

Khmer-native by ear

The boot motif is pentatonic and culturally legible — it sounds like home in Phnom Penh, not a generic Western chime. Accessibility earcons even encode Khmer politeness register as pitch, so blind users hear formality, not just words. Local field recordings (Tonle Sap) power Focus Mode — no streaming, no account, fully sovereign.
