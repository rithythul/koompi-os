The Partner Window

AI Sidebar + Browser, fused

The AI Sidebar and the browser were two views of one mind — so they became one surface. Not a browser that grew a chat (the Google move): a conversation that absorbed the browser. There is no address bar above; you write below, like a chat — you talk to your partner when needed, and it acts once you agree. The web is no longer the container of the experience; it is content inside the relationship.

The page itself — sandboxed webview, taint-tracked, its own quiet page-stack. Clicking links here doesn't spam the stream; only milestones land as cards.
A tapped page card expands to a sheet with a caption, not a cockpit: origin, privacy scope, provenance toggle. To go somewhere new, you drop back to the composer — navigation is conversation. Two sheets can tile side-by-side inside a thread.
One component, four mounts
▸ Rail — Super+A: slim overlay over any app (the old sidebar), instant, resident
▸ Window — the full surface above (the old browser), threads + sheets
▸ Embedded — the same stream docked beside a terminal or editor pane
▸ Pocket — the phone & tablet mounts, where the partner is the device (next section)
Closing a window never kills the partner — it is shell-level, not an app.
The trust seam became a layer
Stream + composer are trusted chrome — consent cards, signing ceremonies, and memory render only here. Sheets are contained content (sandbox → bwrap → Detonation Chamber). The hard rule: a page renders into a sheet, never into the stream — hostile content physically cannot draw an Approve button.

The boldest bet in this document, named: billions of people type URLs into a top bar. The composer accepts a pasted URL at full speed and the "Quick web" thread behaves almost classically — but conversation-first browsing is a genuine retraining of muscle memory. We make the bet because the partner thesis demands it; we keep the escape hatches because honesty does.
The conversation absorbed the browser KOOMPI

Chrome bolts an assistant onto a document viewer — the tool stays a tool with a helper in the corner. KOOMPI inverts it: you live in the dialogue, and the web materializes inside it as sheets and cards. Other OSes give you a browser with an assistant; KOOMPI gives you a partner with the web in its hands.

Talk when needed, act once agreed

The composer at the bottom is the whole interface contract: you speak, the partner prepares, and every consequential action returns as a consent card in the stream — previewed, editable, refusable, receipted. No popups, no hidden automation; agency rendered as dialogue.

One surface ends the silo risk

Six ask surfaces threatened to drift into six assistants; now there is one stream with many doors — the launcher, Files, and the terminal attach their context and open it. The browsing-history debate dissolves too: scrolling back through your conversation is your memory of the web.
