The Thinking OS

AI

Every other computer you have ever owned forgot you the moment it powered off. KOOMPI does not. Beneath the desktop runs koompi-agent-memd — a neuromorphic memory that has watched you since you were eighteen and, across decades, can answer not just what you did but how you thought. It feels context, holds the thread of a thought across a closed laptop, and reconstructs your past with citations. This is what it means for an OS to think across a human lifetime — and every byte of it lives on your machine, encrypted, owned by you alone.

Reboots, new devices, decades — the thread holds. The OS reconstructs how you thought, era by era, and the self you were is preserved beside the self you are.

A lifetime of your inner life — and none of it leaves your machine
Local LLM only. Consolidation runs at 03:00 on your own silicon. If no capable model is present, it waits — it never falls back to cloud. Your episodic data physically cannot egress.
Encrypted at rest. Raw memories live in @data, append-only and encrypted. Sync is E2EE and opt-in — keys never leave your devices.
Cannot be monetized. There is no account, no server, no telemetry to harvest. A self that can't be surveilled can't be sold.
Yours to inherit. Export the whole life as an open, human-readable archive — carried to any machine, or handed to those who outlive you.
Remembered in your mother tongue KHMER-NATIVE

For a Cambodian user, life is not lived in English. The OS keeps your memories in the language you felt them — a note to your ម្តាយ, the grief of a បុណ្យសព, the joy of បុណ្យចូលឆ្នាំ, the way you reason through a problem half in Khmer, half in code. Embeddings are computed on Khmer text directly; reconstruction narrates back in Khmer, era-correct, never flattened through an English lens. When the embedder improves in 2040, your 2026 Khmer memories are re-read from the immortal raw text — not stranded, not translated away. A lifetime OS for a Khmer life must remember the texture of how a Khmer mind thinks, not just translate it for a foreign model's convenience.

It survives its own substrate KOOMPI

Raw episodic text is immortal; the vector index is disposable and provenanced. Your 2026 memories stay re-readable under the 2050 model that doesn't exist yet — and re-readable as your 2026 self framed them. The brain-inspired layering isn't biomimicry; it's the only structure that outlives 30 years of model churn.

Forgetting is a ranking property, not deletion

Decay and pruning touch only the retrieval index — they lower how readily a memory surfaces. The record itself is never erased. Given the right cue, a "forgotten" Tuesday in 2029 rebuilds from the immortal substrate, exactly like a name you recover after twenty years.

It never lies to you about your past

Every reconstruction separates recorded from inferred, down to the timestamped episodic row. An OS entrusted with a human life owes you the truth about it — auditable, citable, and never a flattering fiction.
