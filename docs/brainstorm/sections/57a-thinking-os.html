The one thing every other OS drops on reboot — your working thread — KOOMPI keeps in a store, not in your head. koompi-assistantd's memory engine (Rust, ADR-0004) stamps every episodic record with a timestamp and an embed_model_id; that era tag is the thread. Close the lid, switch devices, or upgrade the embedder years later and the working set, the open loops, and the raw records are all still re-readable — recall always separates what was recorded from what the engine inferred. Everything is local, encrypted in @data, never synced except opt-in E2EE. v1 ships the schema, the store, and the restore path — not the decades-long narrative.

Records kept in the language they were written in KHMER-NATIVE

BGE-M3 (ADR-0006) embeds Khmer text directly — a note to your ម្តាយ is stored and searched in Khmer, not flattened through an English model first. Because the raw text is immortal and each vector carries its embed_model_id, a 2026 Khmer record re-embeds under a better 2030 model without being stranded or translated away.

It survives its own substrate KOOMPI

Raw episodic text is immortal; the vector index is disposable and provenanced. Because every row carries embed_model_id, an older record stays re-readable under a future embedder that doesn't exist yet — and re-readable as it was framed at the time, never silently re-interpreted.

It never lies to you about your past

Every reconstruction separates recorded from inferred, down to the timestamped episodic row — citable, auditable, never a flattering fiction.
