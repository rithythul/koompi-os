Web3 Native

Data Sovereign

KOOMPI is already a self-sovereign system — master key in your keyring, DID in your TPM, exit at full fidelity. Web3 is the same thesis extended to value and identity on shared rails: keys, signing, names, and payments become OS primitives, Selendra-default and multichain-capable. The discipline that makes it KOOMPI: native, never mandatory — the chain is one consent away, and it never gates the OS (ADR-0009 holds: nothing on the critical path, nothing on-chain without a deliberate act).

Native ≠ mandatory. A user who never touches a chain has a complete, lifetime-capable OS forever. A user who does gets wallet-grade key handling, human-readable signing, and agent commerce under constitutional limits — without installing a single extension. Crypto here serves sovereignty, not speculation.

The wallet is the Vault KOOMPI

No extension, no second keyring, no seed phrase in a browser popup. The same TPM-sealed, Shamir-recoverable key custody that guards your documents guards your value — one custody story for a whole life, which is what makes the §62 estate and §70 refugee flows automatically cover crypto too.

Native, never mandatory

ADR-0009 still holds: nothing on-chain on the critical path, nothing written without a deliberate act. "Web3-native" means the rails are first-class when you reach for them — not that the OS drags you onto them. The quiet, chainless path stays equal-weight, exactly like First Boot's identity fork.

Sovereignty, not speculation

No coin tickers in the shell, no NFT galleries on the desktop, no yield widgets. The Web3 layer exists for the same reason the egress gate does: your identity, your value, and your proofs should work without a corporation's permission — in Phnom Penh, at a border, or twenty years from now.
