The dream: your mental context moves with you — pick up the phone and KOOMPI already knows what you were thinking about on the desktop: the conversation, the draft, the open thread of thought, carried in an encrypted Context Capsule over your own local mesh, never a cloud round-trip. The shippable layer: three concrete things — a shared clipboard, file and draft handoff, and phone/tablet pairing. We serialize documents and AI context, and we are honest about the boundary: a running process does not teleport; you see those processes held back, by design, and choose Continue or Fork on arrival.

Honest beats magic KOOMPI

The early vision dreamed of "running processes" following you across devices. They can't — a live process owns sockets and kernel state with no meaning elsewhere. We serialize documents, drafts, and AI context, and we show the two processes being held back rather than faking a teleport. The phone gets the command to restart them, not a lie about migrating them.

Mesh, never a relay

Discovery is mDNS/DNS-SD; the channel is a Noise handshake; bytes go peer-to-peer. When a device is out of range, the capsule waits — we will not quietly route it through a cloud to paper over the gap. Sovereignty means nobody sits in the middle, even when it's inconvenient.

Revocation that actually revokes

Apple and Google "remove device" entries leave old keys valid. KOOMPI ties mesh identity to your Selendra DID and rotates the key on revocation — a stolen tablet loses the ability to decrypt future handoffs. Identity persists for decades; any single device is disposable.

The model may shrink on arrival

Your laptop runs Llama 70B; your phone runs 8B. The conversation context serializes perfectly — the model behind it does not. The receipt says so plainly, so a subtly different answer on the phone is explained, not mysterious. Transparent AI all the way down.
