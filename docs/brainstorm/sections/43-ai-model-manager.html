Honest about its own compute KOOMPI

macOS and Windows hide the model and quietly call the cloud. KOOMPI shows tokens/sec on your hardware before you commit, greys out models that won't fit, and blocks downloads that would brick the machine. Local first only works if the OS tells the truth about what runs.

Degraded never means broken

A $250 laptop drops to Qwen 0.5B under memory pressure — and search, Khmer typing, and the command palette keep working. The OS shrinks gracefully instead of throwing a spinner. Inclusion is the feature: if the cheapest laptop can pick a model that runs, local AI is real.

Atomic swap + rollback Lifetime

Every model update is checksum-verified to a side path and atomically swapped; a worse benchmark auto-rolls back. Over 20 years of model churn, the user never ends up with a broken assistant — the previous good weights are always one swap away.

Hot-swap preserves context

Switching Sailor2 8B ⇄ Khmer-Sealang 3B needs no restart and no logout. In-flight queries queue, weights are mmap'd, and the conversation survives the swap — so picking the right model for the moment costs two seconds, not a workflow.
