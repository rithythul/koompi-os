Death Protocol & Digital Estate

Futures Privacy

You will die, and fifty years of your inner life will be sitting on a disk. The OS treats this as a first-class design problem, not an afterthought in a terms-of-service. You write a will inside the Memory Palace — per-domain, per-person — and the keys to execute it are split among humans you chose, never a company. What you didn't bequeath dies with you, cryptographically.

A will the OS can execute KOOMPI

Cloud accounts die in legal limbo; a "legacy contact" gets whatever the vendor permits. Here inheritance is per memory domain, enforced by key cryptography on your own disk — research to students, journals to a daughter, the rest to oblivion.

Humans hold the keys, never a company

The executor key is Shamir-split 3-of-5 among people you chose. Death is established by dead-man timer plus executor quorum — both required. The OS never infers death from silence alone, and no corporation is in the chain of trust.

Consultable, never impersonated

Memorial mode lets a family ask questions of a life's archive and get the dead person's own dated words back. The line between citation and séance is a hard rule, not a style preference.
