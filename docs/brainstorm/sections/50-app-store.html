Privacy is the ranking signal KOOMPI

macOS and Windows rank by paid placement and download count. KOOMPI's default sort is the Privacy-Review Score — computed locally from requested capabilities, telemetry binaries, and reproducible-build status. The store can't be bought. Curation answers to the user, not an ad market.

Discovery and consent are one moment

Every other store hides permissions until after you've committed and downloaded. KOOMPI shows the exact §42 grant on the detail page, before install. What you grant at install is the entire contract — no app earns new capabilities by surprise. An update that wants more must ask again.

Egress flags from analysis, not the crowd

The "flagged for egress" signal comes from KOOMPI's pre-publish sandbox run and static manifest analysis — never crowd-sourced telemetry. An earlier draft proposed ZK-aggregated community flags; cut (§87) — crowd reporting is its own surveillance vector and a gameable one. Honest AI reasoning, including downgrades, sits beside it: §43 explains why, never just what.

Aging across decades

The AUR-bridge keeps a 50-year-old niche tool installable long after upstream stores forget it, while the strict sandbox keeps that unsigned code from touching your data. Per-app channels let a battle-tested editor stay frozen on stable for 20 years while the rest of the system rolls forward. A Lifetime OS never strands the software you depend on.
