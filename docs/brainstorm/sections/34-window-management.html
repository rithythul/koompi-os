Tiling is deterministic Legible

Unlike GNOME's floating chaos or macOS Stage Manager's opaque heuristics, every window's position is the predictable result of split direction and insertion order. You can read the layout. Same inputs, same result — for 50 years.

AI routes, never decides silently

Smart placement and workspace auto-naming come from koompi-contextd — local, on-device. Every routed window shows the ✦ reason chip with a confidence score and a one-tap ↺ Never do this override that becomes a permanent rule.

Output binding survives unplugs

Hyprland workspaces bind to the output they were born on. Pull a monitor and they re-home gracefully, remembering their binding; re-attach and windows snap back intact. Laptop on a desk, then on a train, then docked again — your spatial memory holds.

Khmer-native keybinding hints

The inline move/resize hint bar labels every action in Khmer with register-correct verbs (បោះ "throw", អណ្តែត "float"), English subtitle below. The shell speaks the user's language first, not a translation layer bolted on.

Floating is an explicit exception

Dialogs, pickers, and calculators float by class rule — and the titlebar says float so you know why it isn't tiled. No window escapes the model without announcing it.

Scratchpads preserve state

A summoned scratchpad isn't relaunched — it's the same long-lived window slid off-screen. Your terminal scrollback, your half-written note: still there at 3am next Tuesday. The lifetime OS doesn't forget your transient context.
