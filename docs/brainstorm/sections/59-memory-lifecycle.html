Memory Lifecycle

AI

How a single perception event travels through koompi-agent-memd — the neuromorphic memory daemon. Perception → Working Memory → Episodic log → Sleep consolidation → Semantic graph → Reconstructive retrieval. Two invariants hold the whole pipeline up: raw episodic records are immortal by default, and the derived index is disposable and rebuildable. Forgetting is a ranking property, never a delete — with one owner-only exception: cryptographic destruction through Ceremonial Forgetting, never automatic, never a side effect. Everything runs on-device — no episodic data ever egresses to cloud.

Invariant A — Durability split LOAD-BEARING

Decay, pruning, and the Ebbinghaus curve govern the derived retrieval index — edge weights, salience, eviction. They never touch raw episodic records. Pruning lowers a node's rank, not its existence. The cue still lives in @data. The single exception belongs to the owner alone — the destroy verb of Ceremonial Forgetting; no daemon holds it.

Invariant B — Model obsolescence

The default embedder bge-small-en-v1.5 will not exist in 2050. Every vector is locked to a frozen model. So raw text must be the durable substrate — precisely so the index can be re-embedded under future models. Each row carries embed_model_id for era-correct recall.

Survives its own substrate LIFETIME

Raw episodic text is immortal and provenanced, so your 2026 memories are re-readable under the 2050 embedder that doesn't exist yet — and re-readable as your 2026 self framed them. The layering isn't biomimicry; it's the only structure that outlives 30 years of model churn.

Models the shape of thought

STDP edge asymmetry + procedural DAGs make how you sequence ideas a first-class, queryable object. The OS can show that your debugging mind grew a "minimal-repro" node between 25 and 45 — evidence, with sources, of who you are becoming.

Never lies about your past

Every reconstruction separates recorded from inferred, auditable down to the timestamped episodic row. Forgetting is a ranking property; remembering is reconstruction with citations — on your machine, owned by you, for as long as you live.
