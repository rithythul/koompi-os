Three authorship marks — stamped at creation, immutable after
human-made

✦ AI-assisted

✦ AI-generated
Produced by the model under your intent. Not lesser — just labeled. The mark you can't forge is the one you never need to hide.

Cloud provenance schemes attest what a server received. KOOMPI attests what a human did — the signature happens at the first save, on the machine where the work was born, with the authorship mark sealed in before anyone could be tempted to revise it.

Proof without disclosure

The Selendra anchor extends the existing opt-in (ADR-0009) from key rotation to personal provenance: weekly Merkle roots, content never leaves the machine. You can prove a document existed unaltered on 4 Mar 2032 to a court in 2076 without showing it to anyone in between.

The negative proof is the killer feature

Deepfakes attack people who cannot account for themselves. A lifetime of anchored creation gives you the rebuttal nobody else can make: here is everything I made that day, signed — and your fake is not in it.
