Your OS as Your Witness

Futures 2.x

The buildable kernel here is narrow and honestly stamped 2.x — a watch item: C2PA-class capture provenance on media created on the device. When you take a photo, record video, or capture audio on KOOMPI, the OS can embed a signed C2PA manifest at the moment of capture — device, timestamp, and an authorship assertion (camera-captured vs. AI-generated). It rides the open C2PA/Content Credentials standard rather than a bespoke scheme, and it is a watch item because it depends on capture-pipeline and ecosystem support that is not yet settled. This is not "the OS as witness to your whole life" — it is a provenance stamp on captured media, nothing more.

The manifest travels inside the file as standard Content Credentials, so anything that already reads C2PA can check it — no KOOMPI required to verify. AI-generated media gets the honest assertion in the same manifest.

On the open standard, not a bespoke chain KOOMPI

Capture provenance rides C2PA / Content Credentials so it interoperates with the wider ecosystem. The value is a signed authorship assertion at the moment of capture, verifiable by any conformant reader.

Why it is a 2.x watch item

Trustworthy capture provenance needs capture-pipeline integration and ecosystem support that are still moving. It is tracked and honestly deferred — not promised at v1.
