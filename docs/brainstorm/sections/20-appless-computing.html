App-less Computing

Futures

In ten years, apps may be dead as discrete things. You won't launch a tool — you'll state an intent, and the OS assembles the right capabilities to fulfill it. "Edit my photo" opens the right tool inline. "Send this to Vathna" picks the channel, drafts the message, and shows it for approval. The app store gives way to a capability graph.

App graveyard
The old icon fades; its capability survives. Apps become invisible engines the OS calls — you never see them again.
Intent over application KOOMPI

You describe the outcome; the OS chooses the engines. Tools become interchangeable backends behind a capability — swap the photo editor and nothing in your workflow changes.

The store dissolves into a graph

Instead of browsing apps, capabilities are discovered and verified locally. New abilities slot into the same intent bar — no launcher, no home screen of icons to manage.

Approval survives the abstraction

Even when apps vanish, the consent tap does not. Any capability that sends, pays, or leaves the device still stops for your yes — convenience never erases the boundary.
