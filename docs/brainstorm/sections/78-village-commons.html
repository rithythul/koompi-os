The Village Knowledge Commons

Futures Data

Your Memory Palace remembers one life. A commons remembers a village. A school, a farming co-op, a pagoda committee runs a shared KOOMPI instance whose memory is owned by no single person — the rice knowledge of elders, the school's institutional memory, oral histories spoken in Khmer — governed by quorum, with every contributor deciding whether their voice carries their name. Cambodia lost a generation of knowledge-keepers once. An OS that lets a community remember itself is the deepest answer this project can give.

Memory owned by a community KOOMPI

The commons is the Memory Palace with quorum instead of a single owner. No steward can read sealed entries, export, or delete alone — the same koompi-assistantd memory engine, governed the way a village already governs its water.

Attribution belongs to the speaker

An elder chooses anonymity, a name, or a seal that opens after them — per entry, revocable forever. Honor and privacy are both forms of dignity; the contributor picks which one each memory deserves.

Against a second silence

Between 1975 and 1979 Cambodia lost the people who carried its knowledge. This time the knowing is written into machines a village owns outright — on its own soil, in its own language, under no one else's key.
