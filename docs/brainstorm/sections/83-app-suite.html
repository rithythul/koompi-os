The App Suite

Apps AI

What KOOMPI builds, what it adopts, and why. On every other OS an app is a silo with its own data, sync, and account. Here the index, memory, consent, and egress are OS services — so a first-party app is a lens over shared memory plus capabilities on the bus. That changes the build/adopt calculus completely: the suite stays small, deep, and composable.

Small suite, deep apps KOOMPI

Because memory, consent, and intelligence are OS services, ten first-party apps deliver what other platforms need fifty for — and every one is unbeatable because of the thesis, not despite it. An app that can't pass the build test ships as an adopted FOSS citizen instead.

The Cambodia moat

Translate, Khmer-dialect Capture, the land-title Vault, the lunar-calendar Day, Commons — these exist because of who builds this OS and who it serves first. No Valley roadmap will ever prioritize them; on KOOMPI they're defaults.

Apps as views, widgets as glances

The §08 widgets (Notes, Timer, Quick Ask) are glanceable views of these same apps, and every app exposes its verbs on the bus — so "summarize this PDF into my notes" is one capability call, not a copy-paste ritual. The suite composes because nothing owns its own silo.
