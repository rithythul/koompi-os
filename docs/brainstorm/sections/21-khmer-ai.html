Khmer-Native Intelligence

i18n

The first OS where Khmer is not translated — it is native. The AI thinks in Khmer, reasons in Khmer, writes in Khmer. For 17 million people whose computing has always been English-first, this changes what is possible: formal vs informal register (សម្តីច្បាប់ vs សម្តីបុរស), honorifics in business email, the Khmer and Buddhist calendars, and transliteration that respects how names are actually written.

The AI selects pronouns, honorifics, and sentence-final particles to match the chosen register — not by find-and-replace, but because it reasons in Khmer about who you are addressing.

Auto-transliteration for names in English contexts
រិទ្ធី ធុល → "Rithy Thul" (self-spelling honored)
វឌ្ឍនា → "Vathna" (not "Vaddhana")
ភ្នំពេញ → "Phnom Penh" (canonical place)

Khmer names rarely round-trip through naive romanization. KOOMPI learns each person's preferred spelling and reuses it everywhere — your name, spelled the way you spell it.

Native cognition, not translation KOOMPI

The local model reasons in Khmer end to end. There is no English pivot in the middle — so idiom, register, and honorifics survive instead of being flattened.

Register is a first-class control

Informal, formal, and royal registers are selectable per message. The OS treats Khmer's social grammar as structure, not decoration — the way a Khmer writer actually thinks.

A sovereignty argument too

A Khmer-native OS means 17 million people compute in their own language without sending every keystroke to a foreign cloud to be understood. Language independence is data independence.
