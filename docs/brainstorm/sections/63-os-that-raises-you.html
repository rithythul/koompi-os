Not a settings toggle — a threshold, marked with ceremony. The parent's view of the child's machine ends here, permanently and verifiably.

Companion, not cage KOOMPI

Every other OS treats children as a restriction problem. KOOMPI inverts it: the child profile is a developmental arc — complexity, register, and autonomy unlock with growth, designed by the company that put laptops in Cambodian schools.

Literacy through use

With Khmer as a first-class language, the interface itself is the textbook. Icons grow labels, speech grows spelling, informal register grows into formal — reading is acquired where the child already spends attention.

Autonomy is handed over, not leaked

Guardrails don't quietly expire at an arbitrary birthday. They transfer in an explicit, dual-signed ceremony the OS records — the child's sovereignty has a date, a witness, and a receipt.
