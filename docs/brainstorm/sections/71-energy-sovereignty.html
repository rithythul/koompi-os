Energy-Sovereign Computing

Futures System

For a school in Ratanakiri or a farm past the last pylon, off-grid solar isn't backup power — it is the grid. So the OS treats energy as a first-class posture, like privacy and network. It schedules its own metabolism around the harvest: deep memory consolidation when the sun is paying, honest rationing when it isn't. And it never converts scarcity into a cloud bill.

Every daemon reads the same lattice. koompi-agent-memd lowers its sleep tiers, koompi-contextd pauses re-indexing, the shell dims its animations — one posture, system-wide, visible in the bar. When the sun returns, the OS quietly catches up on its dreaming.

Metabolism follows the sun KOOMPI

Memory consolidation is energy-hungry but never urgent — the perfect load to shift. The OS runs T2/T3 sleep work when the battery is full and the panel is producing, turning surplus solar watts directly into a better-organized mind.

Honest budgets, real choices

"Low power mode" hides the trade-off; KOOMPI states it: four hours of thinking or nine of typing. The user — not a heuristic — decides what today's electrons are for. On UHD 620-class hardware with a small panel, that honesty is the feature.

Scarcity never becomes surveillance

The cheapest response to a dying battery is "run it in the cloud" — and that is exactly the ratchet ADR-0001 forbids. Work deferred under rationing waits for local power. Degrade local, never silent cloud, even at 9%.
