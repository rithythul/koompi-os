Cognitive Sovereignty

Futures AI

The dark risk of an OS that thinks: fifty years of it and you stop thinking. So the dependence is a dial, and you hold it — per capability, from do-for-me to teach-me. The OS can be made to show its work, teach what it automates, and periodically hand tasks back to your hands. The founding thesis says ten people can outthink ten thousand. That only stays true if the ten still think.

Dependence is a setting, not a side effect KOOMPI

Every assistant ever shipped optimizes for more delegation, because engagement is the business. KOOMPI has no such incentive — each capability carries a user-owned dial from do-for-me to teach-me, and the OS honors the position you chose.

Show the work, teach the automation

Anything the OS automates, it can explain at the level you ask — and in teach-me mode it must. Automation becomes a curriculum: the skill is transferred to you even as the task is taken from you.

Atrophy is surfaced, never exploited

The yearly skills audit names what you've ceded — drifted versus chosen — and offers hand-backs. The data never crosses the egress gate. An OS that profits from your dependence would bury this report; one you own prints it.
